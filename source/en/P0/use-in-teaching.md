# Use in teaching

For some years now, I have been responsible for the course {extlink}`Statistics
and Data Analysis <https://malchiodi.di.unimi.it/teaching/SAD>` within the
Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at the {extlink}`University of Milan
<https://www.unimi.it>`. In that course, learning the foundations of data
analysis is entrusted to a core second-year class that covers both the
theoretical side of probability theory and mathematical statistics, and the
more practical side of descriptive statistics. It is therefore difficult to
find complete bibliographic material: many books excel in only one of these
disciplines, or privilege one at the expense of the others. Consequently, I
have still not managed to find a book that gives these three areas the same
relative weight that I assign to them in my lectures. I therefore distribute
supplementary notes covering both the more _computational_ aspects seen in
class and topics that find no place in the adopted textbooks. As the «critical
mass» of those notes kept growing, I decided to write a book that would avoid
the need to adopt two or more different textbooks, which would in any case
have had to be accompanied by notes filling the remaining gaps.

The contents are organized according to the logical progression I follow in my
classes. The first part focuses on computational foundations, introducing
Python and the libraries I use throughout the book. In the second part,
devoted to data analysis in the most practical sense of the term, I introduce
the topics and tools of descriptive statistics with a view to their direct
use, by writing Python code and analyzing a reference dataset. This is
followed by a part on probability theory, devoted to presenting the main
concepts connected with modeling uncertainty in probabilistic terms. The final
part presents the foundations of inferential statistics, focusing on
parametric inference. Throughout the discussion I have tried to limit the use
of mathematical formalism to what is strictly necessary for introducing the
topics rigorously. Paragraphs whose title is marked with an asterisk include
further details and additional material that can be skipped: they are mainly
topics that, for lack of time, I am unable to explain in class. I strongly
recommend that content to readers who wish to deepen their knowledge of the
discipline, especially with a view to continuing their studies at the master's
level.
